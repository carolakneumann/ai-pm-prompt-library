## Why I Built This

I'll be honest — I didn't start this to build a portfolio. I started it because I was frustrated.

Frustrated watching product teams spend hours writing PRDs no one reads. Frustrated seeing brilliant PMs undersell their ideas in stakeholder reviews because they couldn't get a prototype in front of someone fast enough. Frustrated knowing that the gap between having a great instinct and proving it with something real was mostly just a tooling problem.

So I started solving it for myself. One prompt at a time.

What began as a personal notes folder turned into a system — and that system is this library. Every prompt here has been battle-tested in real product work: discovery sessions, roadmap fights, exec reviews, and late-night prototype sprints. Nothing theoretical. Nothing borrowed without earning it.

I'm the kind of PM who gets genuinely excited when a single well-crafted prompt collapses a three-day research sprint into an afternoon. Who celebrates the moment a Lovable or Claude artifact lands in front of a skeptical stakeholder and changes the entire conversation. Who believes that prototyping isn't just a dev skill — it's a thinking skill, and every PM should be doing it.

This library is how I bring that energy into my everyday work.

## What's Inside — and Why It Matters

Hiring managers and senior PMs ask about prompt libraries in interviews now. Not as a trivia question — as a real signal. <br> Do you know how to leverage AI deliberately, or are you just winging it with ChatGPT?

The difference between a PM who gets AI-native and one who doesn't isn't intelligence — it's intentional practice. A structured prompt library is the artifact of that practice.

Inspired by the open-source spirit of [Aakash Gupta's PM prompt work](https://github.com/aakashg) — 82 prompts across 12 PM categories — and [Dean Peters' guided generation approach](https://github.com/deanpeters/product-manager-prompts) — which treats prompts as teaching artifacts, not just templates — I built this library to reflect how I actually work:

- Prompts with context — not just "write a PRD," but why this structure, when to use it, what to adjust
- Prototype-first thinking — prompts designed to get something in front of people faster
- Cross-functional fluency — for discovery, strategy, stakeholder communication, and execution
- Works with Claude, ChatGPT, and Gemini — so your workflow, not mine, drives the choice
