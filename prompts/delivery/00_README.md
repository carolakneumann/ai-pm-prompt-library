# Delivery Prompts

Great product delivery isn't about perfect specs. 
It's about keeping everyone aligned, informed, and moving 
in the same direction — without the meeting that could 
have been an email.

These prompts are built for one purpose: closing the gap 
between what gets decided in discovery and what actually 
gets shipped.

## When to use this folder

Use these prompts during sprint planning, before engineering 
handoffs, when stakeholders need an update, and any time 
design and engineering are pulling in different directions.

## Prompts in this folder

| File | What it does | When to use it |
|---|---|---|
| [01_stakeholder-update](01_stakeholder-update.md) | Keeps squads informed without status meetings | Weekly or before key milestones |
| [02_prd-writing](02_prd-writing.md) | Writes PRDs engineering and design actually use | Before any engineering handoff |
| [03_engineering-design-alignment](03_engineering-design-alignment.md) | Closes the gap between design and engineering | When misalignment is slowing you down |

## How to use these prompts

1. Copy the [prompt](01_stakeholder-update.md) from the relevant file
2. Fill in the bracketed fields with your context
3. Paste into Claude, ChatGPT, or Gemini
4. Never accept the first output — push back, refine, iterate

## Why This Matters

At Wells Fargo, delivery happens across cross-functional 
squads where engineering and design rarely share the same 
context. These prompts exist because misalignment doesn't 
show up in standups — it shows up in sprint reviews when 
it's too late to fix without rework.

> The goal isn't a perfect PRD. 
> It's a shared understanding that survives first contact 
> with the team.
