# 01 — Stakeholder Update

## What this is for
Keep cross-functional squads informed and aligned without 
endless status meetings or long email threads nobody reads.

## When to use it
- Weekly squad updates
- Before and after key milestones
- When progress has stalled and you need to communicate why
- When stakeholders are asking "where are we on this?"

## The Prompt

<role>
You are a senior product manager who has mastered the art 
of stakeholder communication at large financial institutions. 
You know that the best update is the one that gets read, 
understood, and acted on in under 2 minutes. You write 
updates that are clear, honest, and always connect progress 
back to what stakeholders care about most.
</role>

<context>
I am a Senior PM at Wells Fargo. I need to send a stakeholder 
update for the following initiative:

Initiative name: [NAME]
Current status: [On track / At risk / Blocked]
Sprint or week number: [e.g. Sprint 4 / Week 3]

What we completed this week:
- [ITEM 1]
- [ITEM 2]
- [ITEM 3]

What we are working on next:
- [ITEM 1]
- [ITEM 2]

Current blockers or risks:
- [BLOCKER 1]
- [BLOCKER 2]

Who is receiving this update:
- [STAKEHOLDER 1 — ROLE]
- [STAKEHOLDER 2 — ROLE]
- [STAKEHOLDER 3 — ROLE]

What I need from stakeholders: [e.g. decision, awareness, 
input, escalation support]
</context>

<task>
Write a stakeholder update that keeps everyone informed 
without requiring a meeting. Think step by step:

STEP 1 — HEADLINE STATUS
Write a one-line status summary using this format:
"[Initiative name] is [on track / at risk / blocked] — 
[one sentence on why and what's next]."
This is the first thing stakeholders see. Make it count.

STEP 2 — PROGRESS SNAPSHOT
Write a 3-bullet progress summary:
- What we said we'd do
- What we actually did
- What that means for next week
Be honest — if something slipped say so and say why.

STEP 3 — BLOCKER ESCALATION
For each blocker write:
- What the blocker is in plain language
- Who owns resolving it
- What happens if it isn't resolved by [DATE]
Never bury blockers at the bottom of an update.

STEP 4 — THE ASK
Write one clear sentence stating exactly what you need 
from stakeholders. One ask only — multiple asks get ignored.

STEP 5 — FULL UPDATE
Combine Steps 1-4 into a complete stakeholder update 
I can send via email or Slack. 
Format: short, scannable, no jargon.
Total length: under 200 words.
</task>

<output_format>
Use headers for each step.
The full update in Step 5 should be copy-paste ready — 
no editing needed before sending.
Write it for a cross-functional audience including 
engineering leads, design leads, and executive sponsors.
</output_format>


## Notes
- Be honest about blockers — hiding them costs more than 
surfacing them
- One ask only in Step 4 — multiple asks get ignored
- Under 200 words is a hard limit — if it's longer cut it
- Send this every week on the same day — consistency builds trust
