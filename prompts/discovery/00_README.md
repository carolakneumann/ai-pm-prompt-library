# Discovery Prompts

Discovery is where product decisions are won or lost. 
These prompts are built for one purpose: making sure you're 
solving the right problem before you commit a single resource to it.

## When to use this folder

Use these prompts at the start of any new initiative, 
before writing a PRD, before sprint planning, and after 
every round of customer interviews.

## Prompts in this folder

| File | What it does | When to use it |
|---|---|---|
| [01_opportunity-framing](01_opportunity-framing.md) | Reframes a feature request as a real opportunity | When a request lands in your inbox |
| [02_weekly-synthesis](02_weekly-synthesis.md) | Turns interview notes into backlog signals | After every discovery session |
| [03_problem-statement](03_problem-statement.md) | Writes a rigorous problem statement | Before any brief or PRD |

## How to use these prompts

1. Copy the prompt from the relevant file
2. Fill in the bracketed fields with your context
3. Paste into Claude, ChatGPT, or Gemini
4. Never accept the first output — push back, refine, iterate

## Why This Approach Works for Me

I'm Carol, a Senior PM , and I built this library 
because I got tired of watching great product instincts die in 
backlog refinement meetings.

Doodle Alive was my turning point. Seeing how a team dismantled 
a feature factory mindset in real time — not by adding more process, 
but by getting curious faster — completely changed how I approach 
discovery. The biggest shift for me was realizing that the gap between 
a good idea and a shipped product isn't execution. It's the courage 
to put something rough in front of a real person before you think 
it's ready.

That's what these prompts are built around. Not perfect briefs. 
Not polished PRDs. Getting from assumption to evidence as fast as 
possible — with a prototype in hand, not a slide deck.

> The best discovery isn't the most research. 
> It's the fastest path from assumption to evidence.
