# Strategy Prompts

Roadmap decisions don't fail in execution. 
They fail in the room — when you can't defend your priorities 
to an executive sponsor who has a different agenda.

These prompts are built for one purpose: making sure you walk 
into every strategy conversation with evidence, not opinions.

## When to use this folder

Use these prompts before roadmap reviews, executive presentations, 
quarterly planning, and any conversation where you need to defend 
what's on the roadmap and why.

## Prompts in this folder

| File | What it does | When to use it |
|---|---|---|
| [01_roadmap-prioritization](01_roadmap-prioritization.md) | Builds a defensible roadmap narrative | Before any roadmap review |
| [02_rice-scoring](02_rice-scoring.md) | Turns priorities into evidence-backed RICE scores | When prioritization is being challenged |
| [03_executive-stakeholder-alignment](03_executive-stakeholder-alignment.md) | Gets exec buy-in before the room turns | Before executive presentations |

## How to use these prompts

1. Copy the [prompt](01_roadmap-prioritization.md) from the relevant file
2. Fill in the bracketed fields with your context
3. Paste into Claude, ChatGPT, or Gemini
4. Never accept the first output — push back, refine, iterate

## Why This Matters

At Wells Fargo, getting executive sponsors aligned on roadmap 
decisions isn't just a communication challenge — it's a product 
discipline. These prompts exist because the best roadmap doesn't 
win. The best-communicated roadmap does.

> The goal isn't a perfect roadmap. 
> It's a roadmap everyone understands and trusts enough to act on.
